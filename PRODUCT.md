# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Retro enthusiasts who know the source material — they've played Snake before and come for the atmosphere. They play in short sessions in a browser, usually alone, drawn by the neon/arcade aesthetic rather than deep progression systems. Score competition is informal and self-directed.

## Product Purpose

A browser-based Snake game with a neon arcade identity built on Three.js. The game mechanic is classic; the differentiator is the experience — responsive procedural sound, particle effects, bloom, and a tactile visual language rooted in early vector arcade machines (Tron, Tempest, Asteroids Deluxe). Building toward online multiplayer via WebSockets.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Atmospheric. Mechanical. The feel of a vector CRT in a dark room — not cute, not corporate, not lo-fi. Every element earns its brightness.

## Anti-references

- Casual mobile clones: flat pastel tiles, soft corners, banner ads, life systems
- Vaporwave oversaturation: purple/pink/cyan flood, synthwave clichés, lo-fi nostalgia
- Dark SaaS/dashboard: polished but corporate, feels like a monitoring tool not a game
- Pixel art retro: 8-bit sprites and chiptune — wrong era entirely

## Design Principles

1. **Earned brightness.** Emissive glow and bloom are rewards, not defaults. Only what matters most should visually pulse.
2. **Sound completes the picture.** Every game event has a corresponding audio response. Silence is never neutral.
3. **Tension through speed.** The experience should feel increasingly precarious as score rises — not just faster, but more alive.
4. **Nothing decorative without function.** Particles exist to mark events. Grid lines exist to guide spatial reasoning. If an element doesn't serve the player, remove it.
5. **Atmosphere over information.** The HUD should recede. The world should pull the player forward.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. Keyboard-only play (arrow keys + WASD) is the primary control scheme. No reliance on color alone for game-critical information. Mute toggle available in settings.
